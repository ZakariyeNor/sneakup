What data do we collect?

We collect various types of personal information to provide and improve our services to you. This includes your name, email address, phone number, shipping and billing addresses, payment information such as credit card details (processed securely through Stripe), and any other data you provide while interacting with our website or customer support. We also collect data related to your orders and browsing behavior on our site to enhance your shopping experience.

How do we collect your data?

Your data is collected primarily through the information you provide directly when you register an account, place an order, subscribe to our newsletter, or contact us via forms on our site. Additionally, we receive data from third-party payment processors and analytics tools that help us understand how you interact with our platform.

How will we use your data?

The personal information you provide is used to fulfill your orders, process payments, communicate important updates regarding your purchases, and offer customer support. We also use your data to improve our website functionality, customize your user experience, and comply with legal obligations. We do not sell or rent your information to third parties.

How do we store your data?

We take the security of your personal data seriously. All sensitive data, such as payment information, is encrypted and processed securely through trusted third-party services like Stripe. Other personal information is stored on secure servers with limited access only to authorized personnel. We regularly review our security practices to prevent unauthorized access, data breaches, or loss.

Marketing

We respect your privacy and will never send marketing emails without your explicit consent. If you opt-in to receive promotional communications, you can unsubscribe at any time using the link provided in every email. We use your contact details only for the purposes you agree to and do not share them with third-party marketing agencies.

What are your data protection rights?

You have the right to access the personal data we hold about you, request corrections to any inaccuracies, and ask for deletion where applicable. You may also object to our use of your data or request restrictions on processing. To exercise these rights, please contact us at support@sneakup.example.com. We strive to respond to all requests promptly and in accordance with applicable data protection laws.

What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files that websites place on your device to help remember your preferences and improve your browsing experience. They can track website usage, save login information, and store session details.

How do we use cookies?

We want to be transparent with you — we do not use cookies on our website. This means we do not track your browsing or collect data through cookies or similar technologies.

How to manage your cookies

Since we do not use cookies, you do not need to take any action to manage cookie preferences or settings on our site. For other websites you visit, you can usually control cookie settings through your browser preferences or privacy tools.

Privacy policies of other websites

Our website may contain links to other sites for your convenience or reference. We are not responsible for the privacy practices or content of those third-party sites. We encourage you to review their privacy policies before submitting any personal data.

Changes to our privacy policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or legal requirements. Any updates will be posted on this page with a revised date, so please check back periodically.

How to contact us

If you have any questions or concerns about your personal data or our privacy practices, please contact our Data Protection Officer via email at support@sneakup.example.com or by mail at Sneakup HQ, 123 Sneaker Street, Göteborg, Sweden. We are committed to addressing your concerns as quickly and transparently as possible.

How to contact the appropriate authorities

If you believe that your data protection rights have been violated or you are dissatisfied with how we have handled your personal data, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the relevant data protection authority. For residents of the European Union, this would typically be your national Data Protection Authority. Contact details for these authorities are available online and vary by country.
